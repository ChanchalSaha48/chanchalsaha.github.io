Research Interests

My research focuses on developing robust machine learning models for real-world sensing systems and medical imaging. Core interests include:

  • Deep Learning for Medical Image Analysis (CNN, Transfer Learning)
  • Mechanical Signal Processing and Condition Monitoring
  • Interpretable AI and Feature Engineering
  • Predictive Maintenance using Machine Learning and Reinforcement Learning

Methods & Tools

Signal processing (FFT, Wavelet), time-frequency analysis, spectrogram-based CNNs, 1D-CNN, LSTM, Autoencoders, PyTorch/TensorFlow, MATLAB for signal analysis.

Research Experience

First author of a Q1 Heliyon paper on plasmonic sensor modeling using machine learning. Developed models for cancer histopathology classification using ResNet50 and EfficientNetB0. Experienced in data preprocessing, augmentation, transfer learning, and model interpretability.

Current & Future Research Direction

I am currently working on deep learning and natural language processing applications in the healthcare domain. My ongoing work includes cancer image classification tasks such as brain cancer MRI, lung and colon cancer classification, and breast cancer histopathology analysis using transfer learning and fine-tuning (ResNet50, EfficientNetB0, VGG). These projects aim to improve diagnostic accuracy under small-data constraints by leveraging domain-specific augmentation and interpretable CNN architectures.

In parallel, I am working on NLP-based mental health analysis using classical ML models (Logistic Regression, Random Forest, Naive Bayes) and deep learning (LSTM). This work focuses on understanding emotional and psychological patterns in text data and developing early-stage digital mental-health risk detection tools.

My future direction is shifting toward transformer-based models and Large Language Models (LLMs) for clinical text understanding, medical report summarization, and explainable medical reasoning. I plan to explore BERT, RoBERTa, and domain-specific transformer architectures, with the goal of integrating them into practical healthcare AI systems.
